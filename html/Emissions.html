Emissions

Carbon dioxide (CO2) makes up the vast majority of greenhouse gas emissions. These gases are released during the combustion of fossil fuels, such as coal, oil, and natural gas, to produce electricity. Carbon emissions are a large contributor to global warming. These emissions are released into the air, therefore, damaging the atmosphere which protects Earth’s surface from the sun. There are all types of carbon emissions produced from numerous different sources. Please see below for a breakdown of the different sectors of carbon emissions. These charts demonstrate the number of emissions in (million metric tons) over the years from 1973 – 2016. The charts represent the top five largest contributors of emissions in the world – United States, China, Russia, Japan, and India.
